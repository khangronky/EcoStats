Mission Data FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Question 1

How do you see the data on the website?

Answer 1

The landing page will be the first page you see when you open the website. Click on the “Go to data” button to jump to the table tab.

Here you can choose either the basic mode for regular viewers or advanced mode for researchers and scientists. Click on your preferred mode.

Question 2

How to use the basic mode of the website?

Answer 2

The basic mode shows the temperature and population change between two selected years. There are 2 text boxes that you can enter the year range*. You can select the drop box to view by either World, Country, State or City**. You can sort the table by Ascending or Descending. Click the “Apply” button and the table will show your desired results.

*Note that the recorded temperature ranges from 1750 to 2013, while the recorded population ranges from 1960 to 2013, so keep in mind when you enter the year range.

**To choose the city you want to view, you first need to select the country of that city.

Question 3

How to use the advance mode of the website?

Answer 3

The advanced mode is split into 2 views: the Difference in average temperature between a year range and the Similarity in temperature and population between a chosen country and other countries with similar result. You can switch between them with a button on the top right corner. You can choose starting years (Up to 5 starting points) and year range (ex: 10 years, 20 years...). You can view the results by World, Country, State and City like the basic mode.

*Note that the Average difference mode, you can choose the sort category depending on what view you are choosing. You can sort the results by Ascending or Descending.

**In the Similarity mode, you can choose the Similar category (Temperature, Population or Both) and the Number of similar results (Ex: If you type 6 then the table will show 6 most similar results compared to the original).
